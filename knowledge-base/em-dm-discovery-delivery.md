Discovery decides whether you build the right thing; delivery decides whether you build it fast and predictably. Both halves below, primary sources only.

## Product Discovery — SVPG / Marty Cagan

- **[SVPG Articles](https://www.svpg.com/articles/)** — Silicon Valley Product Group's free article archive by Marty Cagan and partners: product discovery, strategy, empowered teams, product operating model. The reference corpus for how strong product organizations actually work — and the vocabulary your product counterparts use.
- **[INSPIRED: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love](https://www.svpg.com/books/inspired-how-to-create-tech-products-customers-love-2nd-edition/)** — Cagan's canonical book on how the best product companies structure teams and run discovery (2nd edition). The baseline text for judging whether your org ships outcomes or just runs a feature factory.
- **[EMPOWERED: Ordinary People, Extraordinary Products](https://www.svpg.com/books/empowered-ordinary-people-extraordinary-products/)** — Cagan and Chris Jones on the leadership side: coaching, staffing, product strategy, and team objectives for genuinely empowered teams. Directly applicable to how a CTO sets up team autonomy versus roadmap command-and-control.

## Continuous Discovery — Teresa Torres

- **[Product Talk](https://www.producttalk.org/)** — Teresa Torres' site: the home of continuous discovery practice, with a deep blog archive on interviewing, assumption testing, and outcome-driven teams. The most operational discovery resource available for free.
- **[Continuous Discovery Habits (book)](https://www.producttalk.org/continuous-discovery-habits/)** — Torres' book turning discovery from a phase into a weekly habit for the product trio (PM, design, engineering). Valuable to a CTO because it gives engineers a first-class seat in discovery instead of a ticket queue.
- **[Opportunity Solution Trees](https://www.producttalk.org/opportunity-solution-tree/)** — Torres' definitive guide to her core visual tool: mapping outcome → opportunities → solutions → experiments. A practical instrument for keeping discovery honest and traceable to a business outcome.

## Story Mapping & Dual-Track — Jeff Patton

- **[User Story Mapping](https://jpattonassociates.com/story-mapping/)** — Jeff Patton's own guide to story mapping: organizing work by the user's journey instead of a flat backlog. The single best cure for backlog-grooming myopia; the companion to his O'Reilly book of the same name.
- **[Dual Track Development](https://jpattonassociates.com/dual-track-development/)** — Patton's definitive explanation of dual-track: discovery and delivery as two concurrent, interleaved tracks of one team's work — not two teams and not a phase gate. Essential for wiring discovery into a delivery cadence without derailing it.

## Product Thinking & Culture

- **[The Beautiful Mess — John Cutler](https://cutlefish.substack.com/)** — Cutler's newsletter on the messy reality of cross-functional product development: prioritization, org dynamics, measurement traps. The best working vocabulary for diagnosing product-org dysfunction before it shows up in the numbers.
- **[12 Signs You're Working in a Feature Factory](https://cutle.fish/blog/12-signs-youre-working-in-a-feature-factory)** — Cutler's most-cited essay: a concrete diagnostic checklist for output-over-outcome organizations. Useful as a periodic self-audit for your own delivery org.
- **[Lenny's Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/)** — Lenny Rachitsky's widely respected newsletter on product, growth, and org practice, heavy on benchmarks and practitioner interviews. Good for calibrating your product org against how top companies actually operate.

## Delivery Research — DORA & SPACE

- **[DORA](https://dora.dev/)** — Google Cloud's DevOps Research and Assessment program: the four key metrics (lead time, deploy frequency, change fail rate, recovery time), capability catalog, and quick check. The de facto standard for measuring software delivery performance.
- **[DORA Research](https://dora.dev/research/)** — The methodology and year-by-year Accelerate State of DevOps reports behind DORA's claims. Read this to use DORA as science rather than scoreboard — including what the metrics do and don't predict.
- **[Accelerate — Forsgren, Humble, Kim](https://itrevolution.com/product/accelerate/)** — The book presenting the research linking delivery performance to organizational performance, with 24 driving capabilities. The strongest evidence-based ammunition a CTO has for investing in technical excellence.
- **[The SPACE of Developer Productivity](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/the-space-of-developer-productivity-theres-more-to-it-than-you-think/)** — Forsgren, Storey, et al.'s framework: productivity across Satisfaction, Performance, Activity, Communication, Efficiency. The standard rebuttal to single-metric developer productivity schemes (commit counts, AI-generated-LOC dashboards included).

## Flow Metrics & Value Stream

- **[ActionableAgile — Daniel Vacanti](https://actionableagile.com/)** — Vacanti's home for flow analytics: cycle time scatterplots, throughput, WIP, and Monte Carlo forecasting, plus his book *Actionable Agile Metrics for Predictability*. The practical toolkit for replacing story-point estimation with probabilistic forecasting.
- **[Flow Framework — Mik Kersten](https://flowframework.org/)** — The framework from *Project to Product*: measuring flow of features, defects, risk, and debt across value streams and tying it to business results. Useful language for the project-to-product shift and for board-level conversations about where engineering capacity goes.
- **[Making Work Visible — Dominica DeGrandis](https://itrevolution.com/product/making-work-visible/)** — The standard book on exposing the five "time thieves" (too much WIP, unknown dependencies, unplanned work, conflicting priorities, neglected work). Short, concrete, and the best first intervention for an overloaded delivery org.

## Engineering & Delivery Management

- **[engineering-management (charlax)](https://github.com/charlax/engineering-management)** — An 8k+ star curated GitHub collection of engineering-management resources: 1:1s, hiring, career ladders, real company examples. The best-maintained awesome-list in this space and a sourcing ground for EM/DM practice.
- **[The Manager's Path — Camille Fournier](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/)** — The standard text on the engineering leadership ladder from tech lead to CTO, written by a practitioner CTO. The shared reference for defining EM/DM role expectations and grades.
- **[Irrational Exuberance — Will Larson](https://lethain.com/)** — Larson's essays (and books *An Elegant Puzzle*, *Staff Engineer*, *The Engineering Executive's Primer*) on engineering organizations, strategy, and the executive role. The most rigorous public writing aimed squarely at the engineering-executive level.
- **[Team Topologies](https://teamtopologies.com/)** — Skelton and Pais' organizational design model: four team types, three interaction modes, cognitive load as the sizing constraint. The bridge between org structure and delivery flow — directly relevant to platform and stream-aligned team decisions.
- **[Shape Up — Basecamp](https://basecamp.com/shapeup)** — Ryan Singer's free book on Basecamp's method: shaping, betting, six-week cycles, fixed time / variable scope. The most coherent post-Scrum delivery alternative; valuable as a contrast even if you never adopt it.
- **[Software Lead Weekly](https://softwareleadweekly.com/)** — Oren Ellenbogen's long-running weekly digest of the best writing on engineering leadership, teams, and culture. Low-effort, high-signal way to keep the EM/DM reading pipeline full.
- **[LeadDev](https://leaddev.com/)** — The engineering-leadership platform behind the LeadDev conferences: articles, talks, and reports on running effective engineering orgs. Good for current practice (team health, career frameworks, AI-era engineering leadership) from named practitioners.
