# Engineering Management & Organizational Design

High-signal sources for a CTO who operates across delivery, org design, grades/competency frameworks, hiring, and people leadership. Every link was checked on 2026-07-22.

## Curated Awesome Lists

- **[awesome-cto (kuchin)](https://github.com/kuchin/awesome-cto)** — A curated, opinionated list of resources for CTOs with a startup emphasis: management, hiring, architecture, product, and strategy in one index. The single best "table of contents" for the whole CTO job, and a good map for filling gaps in this knowledge base.
- **[engineering-management (charlax)](https://github.com/charlax/engineering-management)** — A large, well-maintained collection of engineering-management and tech-leadership resources organized by topic (1:1s, delegation, org design, processes). Useful as a deep reference when you need the three best articles on a specific management problem, fast.
- **[engineer-manager (ryanburgess)](https://github.com/ryanburgess/engineer-manager)** — A community-maintained list of books, talks, podcasts, and articles for engineering managers and aspiring leaders. Broader and softer-skill-focused than charlax's list; good for onboarding newly promoted EMs on your team.
- **[managers-playbook (ksindi)](https://github.com/ksindi/managers-playbook)** — Concise heuristics for effective management: 1:1s, coaching, hiring, meetings, managing up. Its checklist format makes it directly reusable as a baseline playbook for your own management line.

## Books & Handbooks

- **[The Manager's Path — Camille Fournier](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/)** — The canonical map of the tech-leadership ladder, from mentor and tech lead through EM, director, VP, and CTO, written by Rent the Runway's former CTO. Doubles as a calibration tool: it defines what each level of your own management chain should be doing.
- **[An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management — Will Larson](https://press.stripe.com/an-elegant-puzzle)** — A systems-thinking treatment of engineering management: team sizing, org states (falling behind / treading water / repaying debt / innovating), reorgs, and succession planning. The most quantitative and org-design-heavy of the management classics, well suited to a CTO who thinks in systems.
- **[High Output Management — Andrew S. Grove](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/72467/high-output-management-by-andrew-s-grove-former-chairman-and-ceo-of-intel/)** — Intel CEO Andy Grove's 1983 classic that introduced managerial leverage, task-relevant maturity, and the modern 1:1. Still the sharpest framing of a manager's output as the output of their organization — the mental model underneath most later management writing.
- **[Accelerate — Forsgren, Humble & Kim](https://itrevolution.com/product/accelerate/)** — The research behind the DORA metrics: which technical and management capabilities statistically predict software delivery performance. The evidence base to reach for when justifying engineering investment (CI/CD, trunk-based development, loosely coupled teams) to the business side.
- **[Resilient Management — Lara Hogan](https://resilient-management.com/)** — A short, practical handbook on the human core of the job: understanding what teammates need, coaching versus mentoring, giving feedback, and leading through change. The best compact book to hand to first-time managers, and a solid refresher on communication mechanics.

## Career Ladders, Grades & Competency Matrices

- **[progression.fyi](https://progression.fyi/)** — A directory of open-sourced career progression frameworks and ladders from dozens of tech companies. The natural starting point when designing or benchmarking your own grade and competency matrix.
- **[levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/)** — The de facto reference for cross-company level mapping and compensation data in tech. Useful for calibrating your internal grades against the market and for sanity-checking offers and comp bands.
- **[Dropbox Engineering Career Framework](https://dropbox.github.io/dbx-career-framework/)** — Dropbox's full public career framework: per-level expectations across core responsibilities and craft for IC and management tracks. One of the most complete real-world competency matrices you can borrow structure from wholesale.
- **[engineeringladders (jorgef)](https://github.com/jorgef/engineeringladders)** — A framework describing four parallel ladders (Developer, Tech Lead, TPM, EM) across levels 1–7 with a chart-based format for career conversations. Lightweight enough to adapt in a week for a mid-size org.
- **[career-ladders.dev — Sarah Drasner](https://career-ladders.dev/)** — Open-sourced career ladders for engineering, management, DevRel, and adjacent roles from Netlify/Google engineering leader Sarah Drasner. Notable for clean level definitions and explicit parallel IC/management track philosophy.
- **[StaffEng — Will Larson](https://staffeng.com/)** — Guides and practitioner stories on the Staff-plus IC track: archetypes (Tech Lead, Architect, Solver, Right Hand), promotion packets, and operating at Staff level. Essential for a CTO building a credible senior IC track rather than forcing everyone into management.

## Org Design & Team Formation

- **[Team Topologies — Skelton & Pais](https://teamtopologies.com/book)** — The current standard vocabulary for org design in software: four team types (stream-aligned, platform, enabling, complicated-subsystem), three interaction modes, and Conway's Law as a design tool. The framework to reach for when restructuring teams around value streams and cognitive load.
- **[DevOps Topologies](https://web.devopstopologies.com/)** — The catalog of team-structure patterns and anti-patterns (by the Team Topologies authors) that preceded the book. A quick visual reference for diagnosing why a dev/ops/platform split is dysfunctional before committing to a reorg.

## Hiring

- **[The Holloway Guide to Technical Recruiting and Hiring](https://www.holloway.com/g/technical-recruiting-hiring)** — A book-length, end-to-end guide to engineering hiring: sourcing, structured interviews, evaluation, closing, and compensation. The most complete single reference for designing a hiring process that is both rigorous and candidate-friendly.
- **[Hiring Without Whiteboards (poteto)](https://github.com/poteto/hiring-without-whiteboards)** — A large community list of companies using practical interviews (take-homes, pairing, real-world problems) instead of whiteboard algorithm puzzles. Useful both as a signal of industry norms and as a pattern catalog when redesigning your own interview loop.

## 1:1s & Feedback

- **[The Update, The Vent, and The Disaster — Rands](https://randsinrepose.com/archives/the-update-the-vent-and-the-disaster/)** — Michael Lopp's classic essay on 1:1s: why they exist, the three modes a 1:1 can take, and how to detect a disaster before it detonates. The single best short piece to align your management team on what 1:1s are actually for.
- **[Questions for our first 1:1 — Lara Hogan](https://larahogan.me/blog/first-one-on-one-questions/)** — A concrete set of first-1:1 questions that surface how each report prefers to receive feedback, recognition, and support. Directly reusable when you take on new directs or coach new managers on starting well.
- **[Radical Candor — Kim Scott](https://www.radicalcandor.com/)** — The framework and book behind "care personally, challenge directly," with practical guidance on praise, criticism, and avoiding ruinous empathy. The most widely shared vocabulary for feedback culture, which makes it valuable for setting org-wide norms.
- **[Google re:Work](https://rework.withgoogle.com/)** — Google's open library of people-practice guides backed by internal research, including Project Oxygen (what makes a good manager) and Project Aristotle (psychological safety and team effectiveness). Evidence-based backing for manager expectations, team norms, and performance practices.

## Classic Blogs & Newsletters

- **[Rands in Repose — Michael Lopp](https://randsinrepose.com/)** — Two decades of essays on engineering leadership from a serial VP of Engineering (Apple, Slack, Palantir), plus the Rands Leadership Slack community. The richest archive of honest writing on the day-to-day texture of managing engineers.
- **[Irrational Exuberance — Will Larson](https://lethain.com/)** — Will Larson's blog on engineering leadership, org design, and (increasingly) engineering strategy; the source material for An Elegant Puzzle and Staff Engineer. Consistently the most CTO-relevant long-form writing being published today.
- **[The Pragmatic Engineer — Gergely Orosz](https://www.pragmaticengineer.com/)** — The largest engineering-focused newsletter, covering how big tech and startups actually run engineering: processes, comp, org changes, and industry deep-dives. Valuable for benchmarking your practices against what leading companies really do, not what they blog about.
- **[First Round Review](https://review.firstround.com/)** — Long-form, operator-sourced articles on management, hiring, and company building from First Round Capital's portfolio and network. Consistently deep tactical pieces (e.g., on career ladders, feedback, onboarding) with unusually low fluff for VC content.
- **[LeadDev](https://leaddev.com/)** — Articles, conference talks, and research reports for engineering leaders, from first-time team leads to directors and VPs. A good pulse on current engineering-leadership discourse and a source of curated talks for developing your managers.
