Canon first, critique second — read both. The goal is challenged, working meaning of "agile", not framework cargo cult.

## Foundational Canon

- **[Manifesto for Agile Software Development](https://agilemanifesto.org/)** — The original 2001 declaration (four values, twelve principles, signatories). The baseline against which every framework, scaling model, and consultant claim should be tested.
- **[The Scrum Guide](https://scrumguides.org/)** — The official, deliberately minimal definition of Scrum by Schwaber and Sutherland. Useful precisely because it shows how little of "Scrum as practiced" (velocity, story points, Jira rituals) is actually Scrum.
- **[Kanban Guides](https://kanbanguides.org/)** — Home of the free, community-maintained "Kanban Guide" and the broader "Open Guide to Kanban". The cleanest definition of Kanban as a flow strategy (WIP limits, SLEs, flow metrics) rather than a board with sticky notes.
- **[What is Extreme Programming?](https://ronjeffries.com/xprog/what-is-extreme-programming/)** — Ron Jeffries' canonical overview of XP values and practices. Matters because XP is the source of the technical discipline (TDD, CI, pairing, simple design) that most "agile transformations" quietly drop.
- **[The New Methodology](https://martinfowler.com/articles/newMethodology.html)** — Fowler's foundational essay on why agile methods are adaptive rather than predictive and people-oriented rather than process-oriented. Still the best first-principles argument for why plan-driven software management fails.

## Martin Fowler on Agile

- **[Agile Software Guide](https://martinfowler.com/agile.html)** — Fowler's curated hub of his agile writing: principles, technical practices, adoption failure modes. A high-signal index into decades of thinking, useful as a reference shelf.
- **[The State of Agile Software in 2018](https://martinfowler.com/articles/agile-aus-2018.html)** — The keynote where Fowler names the "Agile Industrial Complex" and calls imposing methods on teams "an absolute travesty". The sharpest insider critique — from a Manifesto author — of what industrialized agile became.

## Scaling Frameworks & PI Planning

- **[Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe)](https://framework.scaledagile.com/)** — The official, full SAFe body of knowledge. Read the primary source before judging it: know exactly what ARTs, PI cadence, and lean portfolio management claim to do, so critique lands on the real thing.
- **[PI Planning (SAFe)](https://framework.scaledagile.com/pi-planning)** — The official definition of Program Increment planning — the cadence-based, all-teams alignment event. Essential reading for any CTO running or inheriting PI planning, and the reference point for deciding what to keep and what to cut.
- **[LeSS — Large-Scale Scrum](https://less.works/)** — Larman and Vodde's descaling alternative: scaling as organizational redesign (fewer roles, fewer artifacts) rather than added process layers. The strongest structural counter-model to SAFe, with deep free content on adoption and org design.

## Evidence-Based Management

- **[Evidence-Based Management Guide (Scrum.org)](https://www.scrum.org/resources/evidence-based-management-guide)** — Scrum.org's framework for steering by evidence across four key value areas (current value, unrealized value, time-to-market, ability to innovate). A useful bridge between agile delivery and the P&L conversation a CTO owns.

## Flow & Lean Thinking

- **[Lean Primer](https://www.leanprimer.com/)** — Larman and Vodde's free ~50-page introduction to lean thinking (value, flow, systems thinking, manager-teachers) — the actual Toyota-derived ideas, not the "lean = cheap" corruption. The fastest credible on-ramp to lean for a technology executive.
- **[The Principles of Product Development Flow — Donald Reinertsen](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6278270-the-principles-of-product-development-flow)** — The seminal book on the economics of product development: cost of delay, queues, batch size, WIP constraints, decentralized control. The quantitative backbone behind Kanban, SAFe's better parts, and every serious flow argument.
- **[ProKanban.org](https://prokanban.org/)** — The community around Daniel Vacanti's flow work (creator of the Kanban Guide, author of *Actionable Agile Metrics for Predictability*). Free resources on using cycle time, throughput, and WIP for probabilistic forecasting instead of story-point theater.

## Critique of the Agile-Industrial Complex

- **[Developers Should Abandon Agile — Ron Jeffries](https://ronjeffries.com/articles/018-01ff/abandon-1/)** — A Manifesto author and Scrum pioneer argues developers should drop branded "Agile" and hold onto the underlying practices. The definitive insider statement that the label and the value have come apart.
- **[Dark Scrum — Ron Jeffries](https://ronjeffries.com/articles/016-09ff/defense/)** — Jeffries' dissection of how Scrum is routinely weaponized against developers (pressure, ritual without technical practices). A checklist of the failure modes to watch for in your own org's Scrum.
- **[Agile is Dead (Long Live Agility) — Dave Thomas](https://pragdave.me/thoughts/active/2014-03-04-time-to-kill-agile.html)** — Manifesto author "Pragmatic Dave" Thomas on how "Agile" became a meaningless marketing noun, and what to do instead (small steps, feedback, courage). Short, brutal, and still the best vaccine against consultant-speak.
- **[SAFe: A Collection of Comments from Leading Experts](https://www.smharter.com/blog/safe-a-collection-of-comments-from-leading-experts/)** — A sourced compilation of SAFe criticism from Manifesto authors and senior practitioners (Jeffries, Denning, Farley, and others). The single best entry point into the serious case against SAFe, with links to each original argument.
- **[SAFe is Not Agile — Jeff Gothelf](https://jeffgothelf.com/blog/safe-is-not-agile/)** — Gothelf (Lean UX) argues SAFe optimizes for predictable output while preserving existing hierarchy, giving "the illusion of adopting agile". Valuable as a concise, specific critique to weigh directly against SAFe's own claims above.

## Rethinking Agile

- **[Modern Agile](https://modernagile.org/)** — Joshua Kerievsky's framework-free reframing into four principles (make people awesome, deliver value continuously, safety as a prerequisite, experiment and learn rapidly). A useful lens for keeping the intent while discarding ceremony.
- **[Heart of Agile](https://heartofagile.com/)** — Manifesto author Alistair Cockburn's radical simplification: Collaborate, Deliver, Reflect, Improve. What one of the original authors kept after stripping away twenty years of accumulated process.

## Curated Collections

- **[awesome-agile](https://github.com/lorabv/awesome-agile)** — A well-maintained GitHub awesome-list (1.5k+ stars) covering Scrum, Kanban, lean, XP, and engineering practices. A reasonable jump-off point for topics beyond this list; apply the critique section as a filter.
