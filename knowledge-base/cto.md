## Awesome Lists & Curated Repos

- **[kuchin/awesome-cto](https://github.com/kuchin/awesome-cto)** — The canonical curated and opinionated list of resources for CTOs, with a startup emphasis: hiring, architecture, product, funding, people. The best single index to keep on hand and mine section by section.
- **[ZachGoldberg/Startup-CTO-Handbook](https://github.com/ZachGoldberg/Startup-CTO-Handbook)** — A full free book on GitHub covering leadership, management, and technical topics for heads of engineering — from technical debt policy to board communication. Practical, opinionated, and written by a serial startup CTO.

## Will Larson

- **[Irrational Exuberance](https://lethain.com/)** — Will Larson's blog (CTO at Carta, ex-Stripe/Uber/Calm): the deepest ongoing public writing on engineering strategy, executive work, and organizational design. Effectively required reading for the modern CTO role.
- **[An Elegant Puzzle](https://lethain.com/elegant-puzzle/)** — Larson's book on systems of engineering management: sizing teams, managing change, succession planning, and organizational debt. The org-design chapters are the payoff for a CTO who owns structure and team topologies.
- **[The Engineering Executive's Primer](https://lethain.com/eng-execs-primer/)** — Larson's book aimed squarely at the CTO/VPE seat: getting the job, engineering strategy, planning, metrics, working with peers and the board. The closest thing to an operating manual for the executive layer of this role.
- **[Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track](https://staffeng.com/book)** — Larson's book on the staff-plus IC role: archetypes (tech lead, architect, solver, right hand), getting the title, and operating at that level. Directly relevant to a CTO who deliberately stays a hardcore IC.

## Staff-Plus IC Track

- **[StaffEng](https://staffeng.com/)** — The companion site to the Staff Engineer book: dozens of interview-based stories from staff and principal engineers at Stripe, Slack, Mailchimp and others, plus guides on the track. The primary public dataset on how staff-plus careers actually work.
- **[The Staff Engineer's Path (Tanya Reilly)](https://noidea.dog/staff)** — Official page for the O'Reilly book that is the other half of the staff-plus canon: big-picture thinking, project execution, and leveling up others, with chapter resources and talks. The best structured treatment of influence without authority.
- **[Being Glue](https://noidea.dog/glue)** — Tanya Reilly's famous talk/essay on "glue work" — critical non-promotable work and who gets stuck with it. Essential lens for designing grades and competency matrices that don't punish the people holding the org together.
- **[charity.wtf](https://charity.wtf/)** — Charity Majors' blog (CTO of Honeycomb): sharp, firsthand writing on being an IC-flavored CTO, observability, sociotechnical systems, and engineering culture. The closest public voice to the "CTO who still ships" archetype.
- **[The Engineer/Manager Pendulum](https://charity.wtf/2017/05/11/the-engineer-manager-pendulum/)** — Charity Majors' seminal essay arguing the strongest technical leaders swing between management and IC roles rather than climbing one ladder. Foundational for career-track and grade design, and for the CTO's own positioning.
- **[LeadDev StaffPlus](https://leaddev.com/staffplus)** — LeadDev's editorial hub and conference track dedicated to staff, principal, and distinguished engineers. The main ongoing source of talks and articles on the staff-plus track beyond the two canonical books.

## Technology Strategy & Org Design

- **[Wardley Maps](https://medium.com/wardleymaps)** — Simon Wardley's original free book, published as a Medium series, on mapping value chains against evolution to make strategy debatable. The core strategic tool for build/buy/outsource decisions and IT-strategy narratives.
- **[Learn Wardley Mapping](https://learnwardleymapping.com/)** — The practical companion: condensed guides, videos, and tooling for actually applying Wardley Mapping. The fastest on-ramp before or alongside the original book.
- **[Thoughtworks Technology Radar](https://www.thoughtworks.com/radar)** — Twice-yearly opinionated assessment of techniques, tools, platforms, and languages (adopt/trial/assess/hold). Both a signal source for tech-strategy bets and a proven format to copy for an internal radar.
- **[Team Topologies](https://teamtopologies.com/)** — Official site for Skelton and Pais's book and ecosystem: four team types, three interaction modes, cognitive load as the sizing constraint. The current standard vocabulary for org structure conversations, mapped directly to Conway's law.
- **[DORA](https://dora.dev/)** — Google's DevOps Research and Assessment program: the four key delivery metrics, capability model, and annual State of DevOps reports. The most defensible research base for measuring engineering-org performance in front of a board.
- **[Accelerate](https://itrevolution.com/product/accelerate/)** — Forsgren, Humble, and Kim's book presenting the research behind DORA: which technical and cultural capabilities statistically drive software delivery performance. The evidence backbone for engineering-investment arguments with the business side.

## Career Frameworks & Competency Matrices

- **[progression.fyi](https://progression.fyi/)** — A collection of open-source and public engineering progression frameworks and career ladders from dozens of companies. The first stop when designing or benchmarking grades and competency matrices.
- **[Dropbox Engineering Career Framework](https://dropbox.github.io/dbx-career-framework/)** — Dropbox's full public career framework for IC and management tracks, with per-level expectations across core responsibilities. One of the most complete single-company ladders to benchmark against.
- **[jorgef/engineering-ladders](https://github.com/jorgef/engineering-ladders)** — A framework-plus-collection repo for engineering ladders, including a chart-based model separating technology, system, people, process, and influence axes. Useful raw model when a matrix needs to be built rather than copied.

## Newsletters & Blogs

- **[The Pragmatic Engineer](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/)** — Gergely Orosz's newsletter, the largest technology publication on Substack: deep dives into how notable engineering organizations actually work, compensation, and industry shifts. The best ongoing outside-in benchmark of engineering orgs.
- **[Level Up by Pat Kua](https://levelup.patkua.com/)** — Curated newsletter from Patrick Kua (ex-CTO of N26, ex-Thoughtworks principal) focused on technical leadership. Consistently high signal-to-noise for the leadership-of-technology beat, with a CTO-coach's filter.
- **[First Round Review](https://review.firstround.com/)** — First Round Capital's long-form publication of tactical, interview-based articles from operators on product, leadership, and scaling companies. The strongest source on the business-of-building side (discovery, product, org scaling) that a full-stack CTO also owns.

## Communities

- **[Rands Leadership Slack](https://randsinrepose.com/welcome-to-rands-leadership-slack/)** — Michael Lopp's Slack community of tens of thousands of engineering leaders, with dedicated CTO and staff-plus channels. The default watering hole for calibrating decisions against peers in real time.
- **[CTO Craft](https://ctocraft.com/)** — A dedicated community, coaching, and mentoring organization for CTOs and senior technology leaders, running the CTO Craft Con conference and an active Slack. The most established CTO-specific peer community.
