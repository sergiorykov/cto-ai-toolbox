# CTO roles and functions — overview

What the CTO job actually consists of in an SMB/SME tech company (up to 250 people, up to 100 in IT) — mapped not from job descriptions, but from the expectations of the four audiences a CTO answers to, with a concrete instrument behind every expectation.

## What this research produced

- **[cto-role-tree.md](cto-role-tree.md)** — the core artifact: business, engineering leadership, line engineers and C-level peers → what each expects → the practice that closes it, with startup / scale-up / SME differences marked. Backed by five research lenses over primary sources (Larson's Primer, Fournier, SVPG, DORA, CTO course curricula, company playbooks incl. the meanings of Avito's).
- **[knowledge-tree.md](knowledge-tree.md)** — the structure consequence: the CTO function map turned into the target folder tree of [knowledge-base/](../../knowledge-base/), with a home assigned to every existing doc.
- **[cto-role-sources.md](cto-role-sources.md)** + `ru/` — the annotated, link-verified source base (EN + Russian incl. the [Avito playbook](https://github.com/avito-tech/playbook)).

## How any CTO uses it

1. **Self-assessment** — walk the tree per audience: which expectations are covered by a working instrument today, which are naked. The gaps are the personal roadmap.
2. **Re-contracting** — expectations differ by level; when the company crosses ~30 or ~60 IT, use the level marks to renegotiate the role explicitly with the CEO and peers.
3. **Practice adoption** — every instrument links a source good enough to implement from; take the doc, not a summary.
