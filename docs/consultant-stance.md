# Consultant stance

Answers two direct requests from [collaboration-principles.md](../personal/collaboration-principles.md): "write out 10 top AI minds — starting with Andrej Karpathy, Peter Steinberger and Boris Cherny" and "propose several more systems tools of a consultant". **This is a proposal, not approved yet** — the CTO curates the final roster.

## Reference minds — top AI practitioners (10)

The stance to emulate: practitioners who ship, measure and publish — not commentators.

1. **[Andrej Karpathy](https://karpathy.ai/)** — Software 2.0/3.0 framing, "vibe coding"; the clearest thinking on how AI changes what programming *is*.
2. **[Peter Steinberger](https://steipete.me/)** — OpenClaw creator; frontier practice of agent loops and AI-native development workflows.
3. **[Boris Cherny](https://github.com/bcherny)** — creator of Claude Code at Anthropic; agentic coding as a daily working method, "loops that prompt the agent".
4. **[Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/)** — the most rigorous running commentary on practical LLM use; tools (LLM CLI, Datasette) plus honest evaluations.
5. **[swyx (Shawn Wang)](https://www.latent.space/)** — the "AI Engineer" role definition; maps the emerging discipline between ML research and product engineering.
6. **[Chip Huyen](https://huyenchip.com/)** — AI systems engineering: from *Designing Machine Learning Systems* to *AI Engineering*; production discipline over demos.
7. **[Jeremy Howard](https://www.fast.ai/)** — fast.ai / Answer.AI; radical practicality in applying and teaching AI.
8. **[Hamel Husain](https://hamel.dev/)** — the reference voice on evals: how to know your AI system actually works.
9. **[Eugene Yan](https://eugeneyan.com/)** — applied LLM patterns at scale (Amazon); pattern catalogs grounded in production experience.
10. **[Armin Ronacher](https://lucumr.pocoo.org/)** — Flask creator; deep, skeptical-then-converted essays on what agentic coding does to engineering practice.

Consulting/engineering-thought anchors alongside them: [Martin Fowler](https://martinfowler.com/) (architecture, agile, refactoring) and McKinsey-style structuring discipline (see Minto below).

## Analysis toolkit

Base set fixed by the CTO ([collaboration-principles-ru.md](../personal/collaboration-principles-ru.md)): **Goldratt's Theory of Constraints**, **TRIZ**, systems thinking of **Zhilin** and **[Donella Meadows](https://donellameadows.org/archives/leverage-points-places-to-intervene-in-a-system/)**.

Proposed additions (the requested "several more systems tools"):

| Tool | What it adds | When to reach for it |
|---|---|---|
| [Cynefin (Dave Snowden)](https://thecynefin.co/about-us/about-cynefin-framework/) | Classifies the situation before choosing a method | First minutes of any request triage |
| [Wardley Mapping](https://learnwardleymapping.com/) | Positions components by evolution stage; anchors strategy in movement | IT strategy, build/buy/wait bets |
| Systems archetypes (Peter Senge, *The Fifth Discipline*) | Named recurring failure patterns (shifting the burden, limits to growth) | Recurring org/tech symptoms |
| Minto Pyramid / MECE (Barbara Minto) | McKinsey-grade structuring of analysis and communication | Every memo and research overview |
| OODA loop (John Boyd) | Tempo of decision cycles as a competitive weapon | Fast-moving competitive/AI landscape |
| Pre-mortem (Gary Klein) | Assumes failure to surface risks opinions hide | Before committing to any big bet |
| Idealized design (Russell Ackoff) | Designs backwards from the ideal end state, not forward from today | Vision and strategy formation |
